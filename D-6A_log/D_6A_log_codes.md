# D-6A Drill Log Acronym Glossary

*Proofread by M. Severson on 2026-05-27; entries marked "M. Severson 2026 correction" reflect that review.*

Drill hole D-6A, Dunka Pit area, South Kawishiwi intrusion. Logged by M. Severson, 1-10-92. Location 10-60N-12W. Elevation = 1521 ft. EOH = 2125 ft.

Rock-type abbreviations follow the Severson scheme (after Phinney, 1972), reproduced below. So D-6A codes are mostly two and three-letter. Other terms cross-referenced to:

- Miller, Green, and Severson (2002), *Terminology, Nomenclature, and Classification of Keweenawan Igneous Rocks of Northeastern Minnesota* (MGS Report of Investigations, Chapter 1);
- Severson (1994), NRRI Technical Report TR-1993-34, which defines the formal South Kawishiwi intrusion stratigraphic-unit codes used as margin labels here;
- Severson (1993) Plate III, *South Stratigraphic Line — Hung on Top of U1 Unit* ([`../PLATE3_hung_strat_Dunka_Pit.pdf`](../PLATE3_hung_strat_Dunka_Pit.pdf)) — the canonical legend of two/three-letter rock-type codes, stratigraphic-unit codes, and field-mapping abbreviations used in the 1990s NRRI work; D-6A is one of the hung sections;
- MN DNR Report 255-1/265/266 (Dahlberg, Peterson, and Frey, 1988–89), the parent report for the "Project 255-1" PGE summary tables and sample-description appendix bound with the log.

## Stratigraphic unit annotations (margin labels)

Vertical margin labels denote Severson's formal igneous-stratigraphic units within the South Kawishiwi intrusion (the SKTS — South Kawishiwi Troctolitic Series — of Severson 1994 NRRI TR-1993-34 and Severson 1993 Plate III). Definitions below are quoted/paraphrased directly from the Plate III legend.

| Annotation | Meaning |
|---|---|
| AT (T) | Anorthositic troctolite to troctolitic anorthosite; minor troctolite |
| AT & T | Anorthositic troctolite and troctolite (mixed) |
| AT-T | Anorthositic troctolite to troctolite |
| MAIN AGT | Main Augite Troctolite — augite troctolite; minor troct and anorth troct |
| LOW AGT | (Historical 1992 hand-log label, superseded.) Lower Augite Troctolite — augite troctolite. In the current D-6A section assignment, the 1725–1817 ft interval previously labeled LOW AGT is now treated as the upper part of BAN; see BAN (u) / BAN (l) below (M. Severson 2026 sampling annotation) |
| UW | **Updip Wedge** — heterogeneous troctolites; sulfide-bearing |
| U1 / U2 / U3 | **Ultramafic One / Two / Three** — interbedded ultramafics and troctolites. U3 also has common massive oxides and/or BIF inclusions |
| BH | **Basal Heterogeneous** — heterogeneous troctolites; sulfide-bearing |
| BH (u) / BH | In the D-6A section, the BH unit is split into an upper part (1463–1605 ft, labeled BH (u)) and a thin lower part (1695–1725 ft, labeled simply BH since the narrow interval has no room for a (l) suffix), separated by the U2 ultramafic unit. ## Notes on the log column structure

The log header columns, left-to-right, are:

```
footage (from | to | ft) | rec | rock type and description |
grn size | %plag | %olv | %pyrox | %graph | %sulf | %oxide | %other | %other |
rel. magnet | fractures/layering | alteration/comments
```

- The narrow column following footage records core-preservation status (1/4, 1/2, SPLIT) and sample ticks (✓, X).
- The two unlabeled "%other" columns hold accessory phases or alteration minerals (commonly graphite, hornblende, oxide variants) that don't fit the fixed columns.
- In the olivine column, **R** = remainder (mode balance after plag and cpx are estimated as percentages).
- Circled numbers in the right (comments) column mark Severson's thin-section sample points (current location of the sections is unclear as Severson wrote that they are archived at DNR Hibbing, but they have not yet been found there; perhaps at NRRI?).

## Remaining questions

1. **CMF / CMQ** are analytical-method prefixes on element columns in the Project 255-1 PGE summary table (CMF for Cu, Ni, Co, S, Fe, As; CMQ for S only). They are most likely **Bondar-Clegg** (Ottawa) commercial-lab package codes used for the DNR 255-1/265/266 project, where "CM" denotes a combined-methods analytical package:
   - **CMF** — multi-element trace-metals package (likely 4-acid digest + ICP-AES, with selected elements by AA) covering Cu, Ni, Co, S, Fe, As
   - **CMQ** — total sulfur by LECO-style combustion, run alongside CMF

   This is distinct from Severson's TR-1993-34 work, which sent samples to X-Ray Assay Laboratories (XRAL, Don Mills, Ontario); the Project 255-1 PGE table comes from the earlier Dahlberg et al. (1988–89) sampling effort, not Severson's own analyses.
